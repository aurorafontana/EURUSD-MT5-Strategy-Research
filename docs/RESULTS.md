# Research Results

## Scope and Caveat

The results documented here are exploratory research findings from demo trading, MT5 reports, trade logs, and reconstructed EURUSD market history.

The analyzed period contains multiple strategy versions and A/B tests. Aggregate figures therefore describe the **research program**, not the clean performance of one immutable strategy.

## Aggregate Trade Study — 1 June to 16 July 2026

A total of 1,823 closed trades were analyzed.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---:|
| Total closed trades | 1,823 |
| Winners | 1,512 |
| Losers | 311 |
| Win rate | 82.94% |
| Gross profit | +€3,883.53 |
| Gross loss | -€5,581.09 |
| Net result | -€1,697.56 |
| Profit factor | 0.696 |

### Interpretation

The most important observation is that **win rate alone is misleading**. More than four out of five trades were winners in this aggregate sample, yet the overall result was negative because the losing trades were sufficiently large to outweigh the accumulated small gains.

This makes loss magnitude and basket exposure the primary research targets.

## MAE Study of Winning Trades

The path of each winning trade was compared with EURUSD M1 history to estimate Maximum Adverse Excursion.

| Statistic | Points | Pips |
|---|---:|---:|
| Mean MAE | 61 | 6.1 |
| Median MAE | 36 | 3.6 |
| 75th percentile | 83 | 8.3 |
| 90th percentile | 148 | 14.8 |
| 95th percentile | 202 | 20.2 |
| 99th percentile | 242 | 24.2 |

The distribution suggests that most winning trades do not require the full original 27.3-pip stop distance, but a meaningful tail of eventual winners experiences substantial adverse movement before recovering.

## SELL vs BUY Winning-Trade MAE

In the analyzed sample:

| Direction | Winning trades | Mean MAE | Median MAE | 90th percentile |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| SELL | 1,296 | 62.3 points | 39 points | 151 points |
| BUY | 216 | 53.4 points | 29.5 points | 125.5 points |

The BUY sample is much smaller and should not be treated as equivalent evidence.

Longer-lasting winners generally required much larger adverse excursions before recovery. This suggests that time in trade may itself be useful information for basket-risk management.

## Static Stop-Loss Sensitivity Study

A static simulation asked: if historically observed trades had used a tighter fixed stop, how many eventual winners would have been stopped before recovering?

Selected results:

| SL | Pips | Historical winners stopped | % of winners stopped | Simulated net result |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 273 | 27.3 | 5 | 0.33% | -€1,840.56 |
| 260 | 26.0 | 10 | 0.66% | -€1,779.50 |
| 250 | 25.0 | 10 | 0.66% | -€1,616.32 |
| 245 | 24.5 | 12 | 0.79% | -€1,576.83 |
| 240 | 24.0 | 17 | 1.12% | -€1,592.48 |
| 225 | 22.5 | 43 | 2.84% | -€1,843.06 |
| 200 | 20.0 | 79 | 5.22% | -€1,982.18 |
| 175 | 17.5 | 104 | 6.88% | -€1,735.68 |

Within this specific static reconstruction, approximately 245 points produced the best result among the tested values while sacrificing relatively few historical winners.

However, even that simulated result remained negative. Therefore:

> **Changing the fixed stop alone did not solve the strategy's profitability problem in the analyzed aggregate sample.**

## Why the SL Result Must Be Forward-Tested

The simulation is path-dependent but not fully strategy-state-dependent. If a tighter stop closes a position earlier, the live algorithm may subsequently open trades that did not exist in the historical sequence.

Therefore, SL 245 should be treated as a candidate generated by historical analysis, not as an optimized production parameter.

## Current Interpretation

The evidence currently points toward a combined research problem:

- loss size must be controlled;
- ADD entries may amplify exposure in persistent trends;
- FIRST and ADD trades may require separate analysis;
- market regime may matter more than a single indicator threshold;
- trade duration and basket age may contain useful risk information;
- late-Friday exposure deserves separate study;
- a faithful event-driven backtest is needed before strong conclusions are possible.
